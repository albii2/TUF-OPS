1.1 Why It Matters

Most salespeople think the job is done when the contract is signed. They're wrong.

The deal isn't closed when the coach signs. It's closed when the uniforms are on the athletes, the coach is thrilled, and the next conversation — travel gear, team store, multi-sport expansion — has already begun.

A smooth handoff creates:

  • A delighted coach who becomes a referral source
  • A program that expands into multiple lanes
  • A relationship that lasts years

A botched handoff creates:

  • An angry coach who tells every coach in the conference
  • A one-and-done transaction
  • A damaged TUF brand in the community

1.2 The Handoff Principles

Principle 1: The Rep Owns the Handoff

You sold the deal. You own the relationship. Production executes the order, but YOU are the face of TUF. If something goes wrong, the coach calls you.

Principle 2: Communication Is Oxygen

The #1 complaint: "I never know what's happening with my order." Over-communicate. Proactive updates beat reactive apologies.

Principle 3: The IRONCLAD GUARANTEE Is Tested Here

Anyone can promise a guarantee during the pitch. The handoff is where you prove it. When something goes wrong, how you handle it defines the relationship.

Principle 4: Delivery Is an Event, Not a Transaction

When uniforms arrive, make it a moment. The unboxing is the first time the coach sees their program's new identity in physical form.

Principle 5: The Handoff Begins the Next Sale

A successful uniform delivery opens travel gear. Travel gear delivery opens team stores. The handoff isn't the end — it's the beginning of the next one.

4.2 Managing Coach Expectations

During proposal:

"Coach, production takes [X] weeks from artwork finalization. If we lock this in by [date], you'll have uniforms by [delivery date] — [X weeks] before your first game."

During handoff:

"Coach, submitting your order today. Production is [X] weeks. Delivery around [date]. I'll send updates at the midpoint and when it ships."

If there's a delay:

"Coach, I need to be upfront — we've hit a [specific issue]. New delivery estimate: [date]. I know that's not ideal. Here's what we're doing to minimize the impact: [action]. I'll keep you posted."

4.3 The Timeline Buffer Rule

Always add 1–2 weeks to the production estimate. If production says 6 weeks, tell the coach 7–8. Arrives early = hero. Arrives on real timeline = on time. Arrives late = never.

☐ COUNT: Total items received matches order quantity

## KNOWLEDGE ASSESSMENT — MODULE 05

Scenario-Based Questions

The handoff isn't theory. When uniforms arrive with problems, a coach is on the phone, and the IRONCLAD GUARANTEE is being tested — there's no time to look things up. These scenarios test whether you're ready for that moment.

SCENARIO 1: The Missing Half-Order

Situation: A volleyball coach's uniforms just arrived. She calls you, and you can hear the frustration in her voice: "We got the jerseys. But there are no shorts. Half my order is missing. Our first tournament is in 10 days. What happened?" You check with Operations — the shorts shipped separately and are delayed 5 more days. They'll arrive 3 days before the tournament. Tight, but doable.

Question: What's your verbatim response to the coach that (a) owns the problem immediately, (b) presents the specific solution and timeline, and (c) deploys the IRONCLAD GUARANTEE in a way that turns this from "TUF messed up" to "TUF handled it"? Write exactly what you say on the call.

SCENARIO 2: The Color Disaster

Situation: You're at an unboxing event with the head football coach and three team captains. You open the first box. The jerseys are beautiful — but the red is clearly too orange.
