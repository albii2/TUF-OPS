PART 1: THE ORDER HANDOFF MINDSET

1.1 Why It Matters

Most salespeople think the job is done when the contract is signed. They're wrong.

The deal isn't closed when the coach signs. It's closed when the uniforms are on the athletes, the coach is thrilled, and the next conversation — travel gear, team store, multi-sport expansion — has already begun.

A smooth handoff creates a delighted coach who becomes a referral source, a program that expands into multiple lanes, and a relationship that lasts years. A botched handoff creates an angry coach who tells every coach in the conference, a one-and-done transaction, and a damaged TUF brand.

1.2 The Handoff Principles

Principle 1: The Rep Owns the Handoff — You sold the deal. You own the relationship. Production executes the order, but YOU are the face of TUF.

Principle 2: Communication Is Oxygen — The #1 complaint: "I never know what's happening with my order." Over-communicate.

Principle 3: The IRONCLAD GUARANTEE Is Tested Here — Anyone can promise a guarantee during the pitch. The handoff is where you prove it.

Principle 4: Delivery Is an Event, Not a Transaction — When uniforms arrive, make it a moment.

Principle 5: The Handoff Begins the Next Sale — A successful uniform delivery opens travel gear. Travel gear delivery opens team stores.

4.2 Managing Coach Expectations

During proposal: "Coach, production takes [X] weeks from artwork finalization. If we lock this in by [date], you'll have uniforms by [delivery date]."

During handoff: "Coach, submitting your order today. Production is [X] weeks. Delivery around [date]. I'll send updates."

If there's a delay: "Coach, I need to be upfront — we've hit a [specific issue]. New delivery estimate: [date]. Here's what we're doing: [action]."

4.3 The Timeline Buffer Rule

Always add 1–2 weeks to the production estimate. If production says 6 weeks, tell the coach 7–8. Arrives early = hero. Arrives on real timeline = on time. Arrives late = never.

PART 6: QUALITY ASSURANCE

6.1 QA Inspection Checklist

☐ COUNT: Total items received matches order quantity
☐ SIZES: Spot check 5–10 items against roster
☐ COLORS: Compare against approved artwork. Natural light.
☐ LOGOS: Check clarity, placement, alignment
☐ NUMBERS: Verify style, size, placement. Spot check.
☐ STITCHING: Shoulder seams, side seams, neck — no loose threads
☐ FABRIC: No pulls, snags, discoloration, uneven dye
☐ GRAPHICS: Sublimation sharp. No cracking, peeling, ghosting.
☐ FLEX-WAIST (if applicable): Waistband flips clean. No bunching.

6.2 The QA Conversation

"Coach, before the big unboxing with the team, let's open one box together and do a quick quality check. I want to make sure every uniform is perfect before your athletes see them."

6.3 What to Do If You Find a Problem

ProblemAction
Wrong size, 1–2 itemsReorder immediately. Expedite.
Wrong size, multiple itemsFull assessment. Call immediately. Expedite replacement.
Color doesn't matchPhotos to Operations. Production error = full reorder at TUF's expense.
Logo placement offMinor: offer discount on next order. Major: reorder.
Fabric flaw / stitchingIRONCLAD GUARANTEE: replace immediately. No questions.
Missing itemsDetermine if separate shipment or missed in production. Expedite.

6.4 The QA Rule

If you find a problem, you tell the coach before they find it themselves.

PART 7: DELIVERY AND UNBOXING

7.1 Making It an Event

Uniform delivery shouldn't be a box left at the front office. It should be a moment.

In-Person Unboxing (Ideal): Schedule when coach and key athletes can be present. Open boxes together. Pull out first jersey — watch the reaction. Have athletes try them on immediately. Take photos (with permission). Celebrate.

7.2 The Unboxing Script

"Coach, this is the moment. [X] weeks ago, we sat down and talked about what [School] [sport] could look like. Today, it's real. Let's open these boxes."
[Open box. Pull out first jersey. Hold it up.]
"Coach — PRO CUT fit. See the taper? See the sleeves? This is what college programs wear. And now it's what [School] wears."
[Hand jersey to coach. Let them feel it. Let athletes try it on.]
"How does it feel? How does it look?"
[Take photos. Celebrate.]
"Coach — IRONCLAD GUARANTEE. If anything's not right, you call me. We fix it. Period. But looking at this — I think we got it right."

PART 9: ISSUE RESOLUTION — IRONCLAD IN ACTION

9.1 The Promise

"If anything goes wrong with the product, TUF fixes it. Period. Not pro-rated. Not 'within reason.' Fixed."

9.2 Issue Resolution Protocol

StepActionTimeline
1Acknowledge immediately. "Coach, I'm on it."< 2 hours
2Own the problem. Never say "the vendor messed up."Immediate
3Diagnose. What exactly is wrong? How many items? Impact?< 24 hours
4Present the solution. "Coach, here's what we're doing: [fix]. Timeline: [date]."< 24 hours
5Execute the fix. Replace, repair, refund.ASAP
6Follow up. "Coach, is everything resolved to your satisfaction?"< 48 hours after fix

9.3 Issue Resolution Scripts

Coach reports a problem:

"Coach, thank you for telling me. I'm going to take care of this. Let me get the details — exactly what's wrong, which items, how many — and I'll have a solution by [end of day / tomorrow morning]. You have my word."

Presenting the solution:

"Coach, here's the plan: we're replacing [items] at no cost. Expedited — you'll have them by [date]. This is exactly what the IRONCLAD GUARANTEE is for. I'm sorry it happened, and we're fixing it."

After the fix:

"Coach, the replacement [items] should have arrived. Everything right now? I want to make sure you're 100% satisfied."

9.4 Issue Categories

IssueStandard Response
Wrong size (1–3 items)Reorder correct sizes. Expedite. No charge.
Wrong size (many)Full assessment. Replace all. Expedite.
Color doesn't matchProduction error: full reorder at TUF's expense.
Peeling graphicsReplace immediately. Investigate root cause.
Stitching failureReplace or repair. LOCK-FIT is a promise.
Missing itemsShip immediately. Verify if production or transit.
Late deliveryProactive communication. Apologize. Make it right.
Defective fabricReplace all affected. Return to vendor for analysis.

9.5 The IRONCLAD Rule

The cost of fixing a problem is the cost of keeping a customer. The cost of NOT fixing a problem is losing a customer — and every coach they talk to.

PART 10: UPSELL DURING DELIVERY — THE NEXT LANE

10.1 When to Upsell

The best time is when the coach is most excited: at the unboxing, one week after delivery, after the first game.

10.2 Lane Expansion Scripts

Lane 1 → Lane 2: ISSUE Travel Gear

"Coach, your athletes look sharp in GRIND on Friday nights. Here's my question: what are they wearing when they get off the bus at an away game? Our ISSUE travel gear — tech suits, duffles, backpacks — same quality standard, same design language. PACKSYSTEM integration. Your team competes in a system. They should travel in a system."

Lane 1 → Lane 3: Team Stores

"Coach, I was at your game last Friday. The stands were packed. Your fans were wearing random school T-shirts. Imagine if they could buy gear that matches what your athletes wear — same quality, same design. Online storefront. Your program earns 15–25% on every sale. Want to see how it works?"

Lane 2 → Lane 4: LEGACY Letterman Jackets

"Coach, your seniors have been in the TUF system — competing in GRIND, traveling in ISSUE. They've earned LEGACY. Premium wool, leather sleeves, custom chenille patches. The jacket that carries your program's history."

Single Sport → Multi-Sport

"Coach, you're TUF in football. What about basketball? OVERTIME collection. FLEX-WAIST shorts — solves the waistband problem nobody else has solved."

10.3 The Upsell Rule

Never pitch the next lane until the current lane is delivered and the coach is happy. Upsell from satisfaction, not anticipation.

PART 11: REFERRAL REQUESTS

11.1 When to Ask

The ideal referral window opens when the coach is happiest: after successful delivery and unboxing, after first game in new uniforms, after you've resolved an issue exceptionally well.

11.2 Referral Scripts

The Direct Ask:

"Coach, I'm glad you're happy. Is there another coach in your conference dealing with the same frustrations you had before TUF? I'd love an introduction."

The Soft Ask:

"Coach, I'm not asking you to sell. But if you're at a clinic and someone complains about boxy fit, late delivery, peeling graphics — mention our name. That's all."

The Conference Play:

"Coach, you're in the [Conference]. That's [X] schools. I'd bet half are dealing with vendor frustrations. If there's one coach you'd feel good about introducing me to, I'd be grateful."

KNOWLEDGE ASSESSMENT — MODULE 05

Scenario-Based Questions

SCENARIO 1: The Missing Half-Order

Situation: A volleyball coach's uniforms just arrived. She calls: "We got the jerseys. But there are no shorts. Half my order is missing. Our first tournament is in 10 days. What happened?" The shorts shipped separately and are delayed 5 more days.

Question: What's your verbatim response that (a) owns the problem immediately, (b) presents the specific solution and timeline, and (c) deploys the IRONCLAD GUARANTEE to turn this from "TUF messed up" to "TUF handled it"?

SCENARIO 2: The Color Disaster

Situation: You're at an unboxing event with the head football coach and three team captains. The red is clearly too orange. It doesn't match the helmets. The coach looks at you: "That's not our red. That's not even close. Our school color is cardinal. This is... orange. What are we supposed to do?"

Question: What's your immediate response — in front of the captains — that acknowledges the problem honestly and preserves the coach's confidence in TUF?

SCENARIO 3: Lane Expansion Question

Situation: One week after delivering GRIND football uniforms, the coach is thrilled: "The kids love them. We've gotten more compliments in one game than we got all last season."

Question: Which lane do you expand to next, what's the specific TUF collection you pitch, and what's the verbatim script?

SCENARIO 4: "I'm Not Paying for Someone Else's Mistake"

Situation: Three weeks after delivery, a baseball coach finds that graphics on five jerseys are cracking. He's angry: "These are already falling apart. I'm not paying the balance until every single jersey is replaced. And I want to talk to someone above you."

Question: What's your response that takes ownership without throwing Operations under the bus and turns an angry coach into a potential referral?
