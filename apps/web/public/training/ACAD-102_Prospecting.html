1.2 The Prospecting Principles

Principle 1: Activity Beats Talent

The best closer can't close deals that don't exist. A rep making 50 calls a week will outperform a "natural" making 15.

Principle 2: Consistency Beats Intensity

100 calls Monday, zero Tuesday–Friday doesn't work. 25 calls a day, every day, builds steady pipeline.

Principle 3: Research Before Reach

A call with zero research is cold. A call after 10 minutes of research is warm. The coach hears the difference.

Principle 4: The Fortune Is in the Follow-Up

80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups. Most reps stop after 2. The rep who follows up consistently closes deals the competition abandons.

Principle 5: Every "No" Gets You Closer to "Yes"

A "no" is data. Track your ratios. Celebrate activity, not just closes.

3.1 Pre-Call Research

Before you dial, answer these 5 questions:

  • Who am I calling? (Name, title, sport — verify current)
  • What do I know about their program? (Record, classification, something specific)
  • What's happening in their world right now? (In-season? Off-season? Budget season?)
  • What's one genuine thing I can appreciate? (Tournament run, strong freshman class, community support)
  • What's my goal for this call? (Not "close a deal" — schedule a discovery call, get an email, learn who decides)

Research Sources:

  • MSHSL.org — school directory, classifications, tournament results
  • School Athletics Website — coach directory, schedules, rosters
  • MaxPreps — records, schedules, stats
  • MN Football Hub / Basketball Hub — sport-specific news
  • Twitter/X — coach accounts, program updates
  • Hudl — team pages

5.1 Gatekeeper Principles

The athletic department admin assistant is not your enemy. She's been protecting the AD's time for 20 years. She's heard every trick.

  • Respect their role. "I appreciate you protecting his time" — mean it.
  • Be human. Use their name. Remember it.
  • Be honest. Don't pretend to be a parent.
  • Ask for help. "What's the best way to reach him?"
  • Build a relationship. By call three, they should recognize your voice — and your respect.

5.2 Gatekeeper Scripts

Script 1: Getting to the AD

"Hi [Name], [Your Name] with TUF Sports. I know Coach [AD] is busy — I'm not trying to sneak past you. I work with high school athletic departments on uniform and gear programs. Is there a good time to catch him for 5 minutes? Or should I send something in writing first?"

Script 2: Shut Down

"I completely understand — and I appreciate you protecting his time. Let me ask: who handles the football program's uniforms? Is that the AD directly, or does the head football coach make that call? I'd love to reach out to the right person."

Script 3: Getting Intel

"You clearly know this department inside and out. Can I ask: when does the department typically start looking at uniform vendors? Spring or fall?"

Script 4: After Multiple Attempts

"Hi [Name], [Your Name] again. I know I've called a few times — I don't want to be a pest. Is there a better way to get on Coach [AD]'s radar? An email he actually reads? A time of year when he's more open? I'd rather do this the right way."

6.3 Cold Call Rules

TUF ACADEMY  //  ACAD-102  //  BUILT FOR THE PROGRAM
TUF
  • Never read a script. Internalize the structure. Sound like yourself.
  • Name the coach first. "Coach [Name]" = not a mass dial.
  • One specific observation. Research is audible.
  • Ask, don't pitch. "How's your current situation?" invites conversation.
  • 4-lane moat within 3 minutes. If they remember one thing, that's it.
  • End with a specific next step. A date. A time. A commitment.
  • Graceful on "no." "I appreciate your time, Coach. If anything changes, you know where to find me."

7.1 The Reality

Cold call → meeting: 2–5%. Warm referral → meeting: 40–60%. Referrals are worth 10x a cold call.

7.3 Referral Request Scripts

The Direct Ask

"Coach, you've seen what we do — PRO CUT fit, IRONCLAD GUARANTEE, 4-lane system. I'm looking to help more programs like yours. Is there another coach — in your conference, in your network — who's dealing with the same frustrations? I'd love an introduction."

The Soft Ask

"Coach, I'm not asking you to sell for TUF. But if you're at a clinic and someone complains about their uniforms — boxy fit, late delivery, peeling graphics — mention our name. That's all. If you're comfortable, I'd love to reach out to anyone you think might benefit."

The Conference Play

"Coach, you're in the [Conference]. That's [X] schools. I'd bet half are dealing with vendor frustrations. If there's one coach you'd feel good about introducing me to, I'd be grateful."

7.4 Referral Follow-Up

  • Thank genuinely. Handwritten note, not just email.
  • Ask for context. "What should I know about Coach [Name]?"
  • Contact within 48 hours. Warm referrals have a shelf life.
  • Update the referring coach. "Coach, I connected with Coach [Name] — thank you."

Coach: Implies he'll choose TUF if there's something in it for him personally.

"Coach, what's in it for you is the best uniform your program has ever worn — PRO CUT fit, IRONCLAD GUARANTEE, 4-lane system. TUF doesn't do individual incentives. We invest in programs, not people. If that doesn't work for you, I understand. But that's how we operate."

You'll have uniforms by [date]. And remember — IRONCLAD GUARANTEE. If anything goes wrong, we fix it. Period. Welcome to TUF, Coach."

## KNOWLEDGE ASSESSMENT — MODULE 02

Scenario-Based Questions

These test whether you can prospect in the real world — not whether you memorized the call structure.

SCENARIO 1: The Gatekeeper Who's Heard It All

Situation: You call a Tier 1 suburban school. The AD's admin assistant answers: "Coach Williams doesn't take sales calls. Period. Email the generic inbox like everyone else." You've called twice before — same response. The AD never checks that inbox.

Question: What's your verbatim third-attempt response that (a) respects her role, (b) pivots to identify the actual uniform decision-maker, and (c) doesn't burn the bridge with the gatekeeper you'll need to get past later?

SCENARIO 2: The "Just Send Me Info" Deflection

Situation: You finally reach a head soccer coach. He's polite but clearly deflecting: "Just send me some information. I'll take a look when I get a chance." You know "send me info" is the #1 way coaches politely end sales calls — and proposals sent without a conversation get filed and forgotten.

Question: What's your response that (a) agrees to send the information, (b) schedules a specific follow-up conversation, and (c) increases the odds the coach actually engages with the material?

SCENARIO 3: The Product Application Question

Situation: You're calling on a girls' basketball program in your territory. It's July — prime basketball ordering window. You've done your research: the program went 18-8 last season, lost in sections, and their current uniforms are BSN stock blanks. The coach is known to be frustrated but hasn't switched because she doesn't know what else is out there.

Question: Which specific TUF product (collection + item + feature) would you lead with in the opening 30 seconds of this call, and what's the exact language you'd use — including a reference to her program's recent success?
