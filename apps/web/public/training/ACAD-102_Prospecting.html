PART 1: THE PROSPECTING MINDSET

1.1 The Math

A TUF rep needs 4 orders per month to hit baseline:

StageConversion RateNumber Needed
Closed Won—4 deals/month
Proposal Delivered50% close rate8 proposals
Discovery Call Completed60% to proposal13–14 discovery calls
First Contact Made40% to discovery33–35 first contacts
Contact Attempts25% connect rate130–140 dials/emails/touches

Roughly 35 dials per week. The pipeline is a math problem. Your activity is the only variable you control.

1.2 The Prospecting Principles

Principle 1: Activity Beats Talent — The best closer can't close deals that don't exist. A rep making 50 calls a week will outperform a "natural" making 15.

Principle 2: Consistency Beats Intensity — 100 calls Monday, zero Tuesday–Friday doesn't work. 25 calls a day, every day, builds steady pipeline.

Principle 3: Research Before Reach — A call with zero research is cold. A call after 10 minutes of research is warm. The coach hears the difference.

Principle 4: The Fortune Is in the Follow-Up — 80% of sales require 5+ follow-ups. Most reps stop after 2. The rep who follows up consistently closes deals the competition abandons.

Principle 5: Every "No" Gets You Closer to "Yes" — A "no" is data. Track your ratios. Celebrate activity, not just closes.

PART 3: SCHOOL RESEARCH TEMPLATE

3.1 Pre-Call Research

Before you dial, answer these 5 questions:

  1. Who am I calling? (Name, title, sport — verify current)
  2. What do I know about their program? (Record, classification, something specific)
  3. What's happening in their world right now? (In-season? Off-season? Budget season?)
  4. What's one genuine thing I can appreciate? (Tournament run, strong freshman class, community support)
  5. What's my goal for this call? (Not "close a deal" — schedule a discovery call, get an email, learn who decides)

Research Sources: MSHSL.org, School Athletics Website, MaxPreps, MN Football Hub / Basketball Hub, Twitter/X, Hudl.

PART 5: GATEKEEPER NAVIGATION

5.1 Gatekeeper Principles

The athletic department admin assistant is not your enemy. She's been protecting the AD's time for 20 years. She's heard every trick.

  1. Respect their role. "I appreciate you protecting his time" — mean it.
  2. Be human. Use their name. Remember it.
  3. Be honest. Don't pretend to be a parent.
  4. Ask for help. "What's the best way to reach him?"
  5. Build a relationship. By call three, they should recognize your voice — and your respect.

5.2 Gatekeeper Scripts

Script 1: Getting to the AD

"Hi [Name], [Your Name] with TUF Sports. I know Coach [AD] is busy — I'm not trying to sneak past you. I work with high school athletic departments on uniform and gear programs. Is there a good time to catch him for 5 minutes? Or should I send something in writing first?"

Script 2: Shut Down

"I completely understand — and I appreciate you protecting his time. Let me ask: who handles the football program's uniforms? Is that the AD directly, or does the head football coach make that call? I'd love to reach out to the right person."

Script 3: Getting Intel

"You clearly know this department inside and out. Can I ask: when does the department typically start looking at uniform vendors? Spring or fall?"

Script 4: After Multiple Attempts

"Hi [Name], [Your Name] again. I know I've called a few times — I don't want to be a pest. Is there a better way to get on Coach [AD]'s radar? An email he actually reads? A time of year when he's more open? I'd rather do this the right way."

PART 6: COLD CALL FRAMEWORKS

6.1 The 5 Opening Frameworks

Framework 1: Big Suburban (Tier 1, 1500+)

"Coach [Name], I've been looking at [School]'s athletic program. [Number] sports. Strong football. Competitive basketball. Most programs your size juggle 3–4 uniform vendors. We do all four lanes — uniforms, travel, fans, tradition — in one system. I'd love to show you what that looks like for [School]. When's good for 10 minutes?"

Framework 2: Small Rural (Tier 2–3)

"Coach [Name], [Your Name] with TUF Sports. I know [School] is the heart of [Town] — I've seen how this community supports its program. I work with programs of all sizes, and I know budget is real. I wanted to introduce myself. No pressure. No pitch. Just making the connection."

Framework 3: Private School

"Coach [Name], I know [School] competes at a high level. Your athletes and parents expect a certain standard. We deliver PRO CUT fit — built to what college programs wear — at a price private school budgets can work with. And we outfit the whole program across four lanes. If you've got 10 minutes, I'll show you the difference."

Framework 4: Charter School

"Coach [Name], I know [School] is building something. Newer programs get to define their identity from the start. We build program identity — uniforms, travel gear, team stores, letterman jackets — in one connected system. I'd love to show you what that looks like before you lock into a vendor."

Framework 5: Metro Public (Tier 2, 700–1500)

"Coach [Name], [Your Name] with TUF Sports. [School] is in a competitive conference with [Rival 1] and [Rival 2]. Your athletes want to look like they belong. We deliver PRO CUT fit — college-program look — at a metro public school budget. Across all four lanes. When can I show you what [School] looks like in TUF?"

6.3 Cold Call Rules

  1. Never read a script. Internalize the structure. Sound like yourself.
  2. Name the coach first. "Coach [Name]" = not a mass dial.
  3. One specific observation. Research is audible.
  4. Ask, don't pitch. "How's your current situation?" invites conversation.
  5. 4-lane moat within 3 minutes. If they remember one thing, that's it.
  6. End with a specific next step. A date. A time. A commitment.
  7. Graceful on "no." "I appreciate your time, Coach. If anything changes, you know where to find me."

6.4 Voicemail — 30 Seconds Max

PART 7: WARM REFERRAL STRATEGIES

7.1 The Reality

Cold call → meeting: 2–5%. Warm referral → meeting: 40–60%. Referrals are worth 10x a cold call.

7.2 When to Ask

TimingScript
After successful delivery"Coach, I'm glad you're happy with the GRIND uniforms. Is there another coach in your conference dealing with the same frustrations you had? Someone who'd appreciate seeing what you've got?"
After positive discovery call"Coach, I appreciate the time. Is there another program in your area facing similar uniform challenges?"
At coaching clinics"Coach, thanks for the introduction. Any friend of Coach [Name] is someone I want to know. Tell me about your program."
During order handoff"Coach, when these arrive and your kids see them — other coaches are going to notice. Who should I reach out to first?"

7.3 Referral Request Scripts

The Direct Ask

"Coach, you've seen what we do — PRO CUT fit, IRONCLAD GUARANTEE, 4-lane system. I'm looking to help more programs like yours. Is there another coach — in your conference, in your network — who's dealing with the same frustrations? I'd love an introduction."

The Soft Ask

"Coach, I'm not asking you to sell for TUF. But if you're at a clinic and someone complains about their uniforms — boxy fit, late delivery, peeling graphics — mention our name. That's all. If you're comfortable, I'd love to reach out to anyone you think might benefit."

The Conference Play

"Coach, you're in the [Conference]. That's [X] schools. I'd bet half are dealing with vendor frustrations. If there's one coach you'd feel good about introducing me to, I'd be grateful."

7.4 Referral Follow-Up

  1. Thank genuinely. Handwritten note, not just email.
  2. Ask for context. "What should I know about Coach [Name]?"
  3. Contact within 48 hours. Warm referrals have a shelf life.
  4. Update the referring coach. "Coach, I connected with Coach [Name] — thank you."

PART 10: THE "FIRST 50 CALLS" CHALLENGE

Your first 50 calls are not about closing. They're about learning the rhythm, testing scripts, building call muscle, and getting your first "wins" (a scheduled discovery call IS a win).

CallsFocus
1–10Just dial. Get comfortable. Log everything.
11–20Test different openers. Find what feels natural.
21–30Focus on 4-lane moat articulation (under 30 seconds).
31–40Handle objections. Practice competitive response. Budget response.
41–50Aim for discovery calls. Target: 3–5 scheduled.

Rules: 50 calls in 5 business days (10/day minimum). No skipping. Voicemails count. Every dial counts. Log every call immediately. Review with Director after every 10 calls. Record yourself on 5 calls. Listen back. Improve.

PART 11: 15 ROLE-PLAY SCENARIOS

Practice with your Director or fellow rep. Debrief 2–3 minutes after each.

Scenario 1: The Busy Coach

Coach: Head Football Coach. Practice in background.

"Yeah, this is Coach Miller. Make it quick — I've got kids on the field."

Model Response:

"Coach, 30 seconds. [Your Name], TUF Sports. We outfit programs across four lanes — uniforms, travel, fans, tradition — one system, PRO CUT fit to college spec, IRONCLAD GUARANTEE. Are you happy with your current uniforms, or is there a better time to talk?"

Scenario 2: The Voicemail Champion

Coach: 4 calls. Always voicemail. Never a callback.

Model Response (Voicemail):

"Coach [Name], [Your Name] TUF Sports. Fourth time's the charm, or I'm stubborn. I think what we do could help [School]'s program. If I'm wrong, tell me and I'll stop calling. If you're curious, 5 minutes. [Number]. Built for the program."

Scenario 3: The "We Just Ordered" Coach

Coach: Head Baseball Coach. Friendly but closed.

"You're about a month too late. We just placed our order. Call me next year."

Model Response:

"Coach, totally understand. Let me do two things: I'll put a reminder for [next ordering window — likely October]. I'll reach out then. And I'd love to drop off a DIAMOND baseball sample. Put it on one of your kids this spring. When I call in October, you'll already know if the fit is right. No pressure. Sound fair?"

Scenario 4: The Rude Coach

Coach: Bad day. Takes it out on you. Hangs up.

"I don't have time for sales calls. Don't call here again." (Click.)

Response: Log the call. "Coach not receptive. Bad timing. Will try email in 2 weeks." Wait 14 days. Send a brief, no-pressure email. Don't call back immediately. Respect the boundary. Try a different approach: attend a game. Send a sample with a note.

Scenario 5: The Super Interested Coach

Coach: Ready to switch. Current vendor just failed.

"You called at the perfect time. Our current company is a disaster. What do you have? What's the price? How fast can you deliver?"

Model Response:

"Coach, I hear the frustration — and I'm sorry you're dealing with that. I want to give you the right solution, not the fastest answer. Can we schedule 20 minutes — I'll learn about your program, your timeline, exactly what you need — then I'll put together exactly what TUF can deliver. When's good this week?"

Scenario 6: The Gatekeeper (Advanced)

Gatekeeper: AD's admin. 25 years. Heard it all.

"Coach Williams doesn't take sales calls. Period. Email the generic inbox."

Model Response:

"I appreciate you being direct — and I respect that you protect his time. Let me ask: who at [School] actually makes the uniform decisions? Is it Coach Williams directly, or do the individual sport coaches handle it? I'd rather reach out to the right person than waste anyone's time."

Scenario 7: The "Send Me Information" Coach

Coach: Head Soccer Coach. Not unfriendly. Deflecting.

"Just send me some information. I'll take a look when I get a chance."

Model Response:

"Absolutely. What email should I send it to? And when's a good time for me to follow up — I want to make sure you actually see it. How about I send it now and give you a call [specific day]?"

Scenario 8: The "We're a Nike School" Coach

Coach: District-wide Nike contract.

"We're Nike, top to bottom. Contract's locked. Nothing you can do."

Model Response:

"Coach, I respect the contract. Not here to fight it. Let me ask: does the Nike contract cover all sports equally? Does it cover travel gear, team stores, and letterman jackets — or just game-day uniforms? Most district contracts only cover Lane 1. The other three lanes are wide open."

Scenario 9: The Small School Budget Coach

Coach: Small rural school. 200 kids. Barely a budget.

"Son, we buy softballs at Walmart. We can't afford custom anything."

Model Response:

"Coach, I appreciate your honesty. I'm not pretending we're the cheapest — we're not. But here's what might help: our team store model. Online storefront. Community buys spirit wear. Your program earns 15–25% on every sale. I've seen programs fund their entire uniform order this way. I'll send you the info. If it ever makes sense, I'm here. If not — I respect that."

Scenario 10: The Coach Who Ghosts

Coach: Great discovery call. Sent proposal. Silence. Two weeks. Three follow-ups.

Model Response (Email):

"Coach [Name], I've been trying to reach you about the proposal. I'll assume the timing isn't right — that's fine. I'll stop calling for now. The proposal doesn't expire. When you're ready, I'm here. Best of luck with the season. Built for the program."

(Wait 30 days. Send a check-in. Try social. Attend a game.)

Scenario 11: The "Call My AD" Coach

Coach: Likes what you're saying. AD controls budget.

"I like what I'm hearing, but you need to talk to our AD. Her name is Sarah Chen."

Model Response:

"Coach, I appreciate that. Before I reach out to Sarah — can you give me context? What's her priority? Budget-focused? Quality-focused? Any vendor relationships I should know about? And if you're comfortable — would you give her a heads up that I'll be calling?"

Scenario 12: The Multi-Sport AD

Coach: AD. Curious. Cautious.

"We have 14 sports. You're telling me one company can handle all of them?"

Model Response:

"Fair question. Let me be specific. Lane 1 — Uniforms: Football (GRIND), Basketball (OVERTIME with FLEX-WAIST), Baseball/Softball (DIAMOND). Lane 2 — Travel: ISSUE, one system. Lane 3 — Team Stores: online, program earns revenue. Lane 4 — Letterman: LEGACY. Each lane has sport-specific expertise. One quality standard. One relationship. Start with one sport. If we deliver, expand. Fair?"

Scenario 13: The Coach's Spouse Answers

Coach: You call the coach's cell. Wife answers.

"He's not available right now. Who's calling?"

Model Response:

"Hi, this is [Your Name] with TUF Sports. I work with Coach [Name] on [School]'s uniforms. Nothing urgent — just following up. Could you let him know I called? He has my number. Thank you!"

Scenario 14: The Coach Who Wants a Kickback

Coach: Implies he'll choose TUF if there's something in it for him personally.

"Look, between you and me — I can make this decision. What's in it for me?"

Model Response:

"Coach, what's in it for you is the best uniform your program has ever worn — PRO CUT fit, IRONCLAD GUARANTEE, 4-lane system. TUF doesn't do individual incentives. We invest in programs, not people. If that doesn't work for you, I understand. But that's how we operate."

(Document. Report to Director.)

Scenario 15: The Coach Who Says Yes

Coach: 4 discovery calls. 2 proposals. Samples tried. He's ready.

"Alright. Let's do it. What do you need from me?"

Model Response:

"Coach, that's great. Let's build it. Here's what I need: roster with sizes, logo files, and confirmation on the design we discussed. I'll send the final order form today. Production is [X weeks]. You'll have uniforms by [date]. And remember — IRONCLAD GUARANTEE. If anything goes wrong, we fix it. Period. Welcome to TUF, Coach."

KNOWLEDGE ASSESSMENT — MODULE 02

Scenario-Based Questions

These test whether you can prospect in the real world — not whether you memorized the call structure.

SCENARIO 1: The Gatekeeper Who's Heard It All

Situation: You call a Tier 1 suburban school. The AD's admin assistant answers: "Coach Williams doesn't take sales calls. Period. Email the generic inbox like everyone else." You've called twice before — same response. The AD never checks that inbox.

Question: What's your verbatim third-attempt response that (a) respects her role, (b) pivots to identify the actual uniform decision-maker, and (c) doesn't burn the bridge with the gatekeeper you'll need to get past later?

SCENARIO 2: The "Just Send Me Info" Deflection

Situation: You finally reach a head soccer coach. He's polite but clearly deflecting: "Just send me some information. I'll take a look when I get a chance." You know "send me info" is the #1 way coaches politely end sales calls — and proposals sent without a conversation get filed and forgotten.

Question: What's your response that (a) agrees to send the information, (b) schedules a specific follow-up conversation, and (c) increases the odds the coach actually engages with the material?

SCENARIO 3: The Product Application Question

Situation: You're calling on a girls' basketball program in your territory. It's July — prime basketball ordering window. You've done your research: the program went 18-8 last season, lost in sections, and their current uniforms are BSN stock blanks. The coach is known to be frustrated but hasn't switched because she doesn't know what else is out there.

Question: Which specific TUF product (collection + item + feature) would you lead with in the opening 30 seconds of this call, and what's the exact language you'd use — including a reference to her program's recent success?

SCENARIO 4: The Objection — "We Just Ordered"

Situation: You reach a head baseball coach in April. He's friendly but says: "You're about a month too late. We just placed our order with our local guy. Call me next year."

Question: What's your response that (a) respects the timing, (b) plants a seed that makes him think about TUF during the current season, and (c) sets a specific follow-up that virtually guarantees you'll be the first call he takes when next year's ordering window opens?

Recall Foundation

  1. Prospecting Math: From 130–140 contact attempts, how many closed-won deals should result per month? What's the conversion rate at each stage?
  2. Territory Zones: Name the four territory zone classifications and the strategy for each.
  3. Cold Call Structure: What happens in Minute 0:00–0:30? What's the rule about the 4-lane moat?

Director Checkpoint

Complete a 10-minute role-play with your Director:

  • The Opening Line: Director says "Go." You deliver a cold call opener for a Tier 1 football coach. Director evaluates: research reference, coach's name first, question not pitch.
  • Gatekeeper Drill: Director plays the admin who's blocked you twice. You deliver the third-attempt script. Director evaluates: respect, pivot, persistence without annoyance.
  • Voicemail Challenge: Director says "Leave me a voicemail for a basketball coach. 30 seconds. Go." You deliver. Director times you.
