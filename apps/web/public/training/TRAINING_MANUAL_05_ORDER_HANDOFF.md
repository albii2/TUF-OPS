## PART 1: THE ORDER HANDOFF MINDSET

### 1.1 Why Order Handoff Matters

Most salespeople think the job is done when the contract is signed. They're wrong. The deal isn't closed when the coach signs — it's closed when the uniforms are on the athletes, the coach is thrilled, and the next conversation (travel gear, team store, multi-sport expansion) has already begun.

**The order handoff is where deals are won and relationships are built — or lost.**

A smooth handoff creates:
- A delighted coach who becomes a referral source
- A program that expands into multiple lanes
- A relationship that lasts years instead of a single transaction

A botched handoff creates:
- An angry coach who tells every other coach in the conference
- A one-and-done transaction with no lane expansion
- A damaged TUF brand reputation in the community

### 1.2 The Handoff Principles

**Principle 1: The Rep Owns the Handoff**

You sold the deal. You own the relationship. The production team executes the order, but you are the face of TUF throughout the entire process. If something goes wrong, the coach calls you — not a 1-800 number.

**Principle 2: Communication Is Oxygen**

The number one complaint coaches have about vendors: "I never know what's happening with my order." Over-communicate. Proactive updates beat reactive apologies every time.

**Principle 3: The IRONCLAD GUARANTEE Is Tested in the Handoff**

Anyone can promise a guarantee during the sales pitch. The handoff is where you prove it. When something goes wrong (and something always does — wrong size, delayed shipment, missing item), how you handle it defines the relationship.

**Principle 4: Delivery Is an Event, Not a Transaction**

When uniforms arrive, make it a moment. The unboxing is the first time the coach sees their program's new identity in physical form. Treat it like the milestone it is.

**Principle 5: The Handoff Is the Beginning of the Next Sale**

A successful uniform delivery opens the door to travel gear. A successful travel gear delivery opens the door to team stores. The handoff isn't the end of a cycle — it's the beginning of the next one.

### 3.2 The Director QA Question

Before any order goes to Operations, the Director reviews it and asks one question:

> **"If this order arrives wrong, what's the cost — in dollars, in trust, in reputation — and have we done everything possible to prevent that?"**

If the answer reveals gaps, the order doesn't move forward until they're filled.

### 3.3 Common Handoff Mistakes

| Mistake | Consequence | Prevention |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| Missing or wrong sizes | Athletes can't wear the uniforms. Reorder costs time and money. | Verify roster. Conduct (or offer) sizing session. |
| Low-resolution logo files | Blurry/pixelated graphics on final product. Coach rejects order. | Request vector files (AI, EPS) or 300dpi+ PNG. |
| Unspecified color values | Colors don't match school. "That's not our blue." | Get PMS color codes whenever possible. |
| Wrong delivery address | Order goes to wrong location. Delayed delivery. Angry coach. | Verify delivery address during order handoff. |
| Missing deposit | Production doesn't start. Timeline slips. | Confirm deposit before submitting to Ops. |
| No secondary contact | Primary contact is unavailable. Can't confirm details. | Always get a backup contact. |

## PART 4: TIMELINE MANAGEMENT

### 4.1 Production Timeline Overview

Production timelines vary by product type, order size, and season:

| Product Type | Standard Lead Time | Rush Lead Time | Notes |
|-------------|-------------------|----------------|-------|
| **Sublimated Uniforms** | 6–8 weeks | 4–5 weeks | Full custom design. Longer lead time. |
| **Screen-Printed Uniforms** | 4–6 weeks | 3–4 weeks | Simpler decoration. Faster turnaround. |
| **Tackle Twill Uniforms** | 8–10 weeks | 6–7 weeks | Stitched lettering. Premium. Slowest. |
| **Travel Gear (ISSUE)** | 6–8 weeks | 4–5 weeks | PACKSYSTEM integration. |
| **Team Store Items** | 2–4 weeks per order | N/A | Print-on-demand. Faster individual orders. |
| **Letterman Jackets (LEGACY)** | 8–12 weeks | 6–8 weeks | Custom chenille patches. Slowest lane. |

### 4.2 The Critical Path

For a fall sport (football) order placed in March for August delivery:

```
MAR 15     Order submitted to Operations
MAR 22     Artwork finalized and approved
MAR 29     Production begins
MAY 15     Mid-production check-in
JUN 15     Production complete
JUN 22     Quality inspection
JUN 29     Shipping
JUL 6      Delivery to school
JUL 13     Satisfaction check
JUL 20     Travel gear conversation (Lane 2)
```

### 4.3 Managing Coach Expectations

**During the proposal, set the timeline expectation:**
> *"Coach, production takes [X] weeks from the time we finalize artwork. If we lock this in by [date], you'll have uniforms in hand by [delivery date] — [X weeks] before your first game."*

**During the handoff, confirm the timeline:**
> *"Coach, I'm submitting your order today. Production is [X] weeks. That puts delivery around [date]. I'll send you updates at the midpoint and when it ships. You'll know exactly where things stand the whole way."*

**If there's a delay:**
> *"Coach, I need to be upfront — we've hit a [specific issue] that's pushing your timeline by [X days]. Your new delivery estimate is [date]. I know that's not ideal, and I'm sorry. Here's what we're doing to minimize the impact: [action]. I'll keep you posted every step."*

### 4.4 The Timeline Buffer Rule

Always add a 1–2 week buffer to the production estimate when communicating with the coach. If production says 6 weeks, tell the coach 7–8 weeks. If it arrives early, you're a hero. If it arrives on the real timeline, you're on time.

## PART 6: QUALITY ASSURANCE

### 6.1 What to Inspect When Products Arrive

Before the coach sees the uniforms, you should inspect them (when possible). If you can't be there in person, walk the coach through an inspection checklist.

#### QA Inspection Checklist

```
☐ COUNT: Total items received matches order quantity
☐ SIZES: Spot check 5–10 items against the roster. Verify size labels.
☐ COLORS: Compare against approved artwork. Hold in natural light.
☐ LOGOS: Check clarity, placement, and alignment on all items.
☐ NUMBERS: Verify number style, size, and placement. Spot check 5–10.
☐ STITCHING: Check shoulder seams, side seams, neck — no loose threads.
☐ FABRIC: Check for flaws — pulls, snags, discoloration, uneven dye.
☐ GRAPHICS: Sublimation sharp and clear. No cracking, peeling, or ghosting.
☐ TACKLE TWILL (if applicable): Letters/numbers securely stitched. No loose threads.
☐ FLEX-WAIST (if applicable): Waistband flips clean. No bunching.
```

### 6.2 The QA Conversation with the Coach

> *"Coach, before we do the big unboxing with the team, let's open one box together and do a quick quality check. I want to make sure every uniform is perfect before your athletes see them. If anything's off, we catch it now."*

This shows professionalism. It catches issues before they become public. And it reinforces the IRONCLAD GUARANTEE — you're proactively looking for problems to fix.

### 6.3 What to Do If You Find a Problem

| Problem | Action |
|---------|--------|
| **Wrong size on 1–2 items** | Reorder immediately. Expedite. Tell the coach: "Two jerseys came in the wrong size. I've already reordered. They'll be here in [X] days. In the meantime, use the closest size we have." |
| **Wrong size on multiple items** | Full assessment. Call the coach immediately. Determine if usable until replacements arrive. Expedite replacement order. |
| **Color doesn't match** | Take photos. Send to Operations. Determine root cause (artwork error vs. production error). If production error: full reorder at TUF's expense. |
| **Logo placement off** | Minor: offer discount on next order. Major: reorder. |
| **Fabric flaw / stitching issue** | IRONCLAD GUARANTEE: replace immediately. No questions. |
| **Missing items** | Determine if shipped separately or missed in production. Expedite missing items. |

### 6.4 The QA Rule

> **If you find a problem, you tell the coach before they find it themselves.**

Discovering and addressing an issue proactively builds trust. The coach discovering an issue on their own erodes trust. Always be the bearer of bad news — it proves you're paying attention.

## PART 7: DELIVERY AND UNBOXING

### 7.1 Making Delivery an Event

Uniform delivery shouldn't be a box left at the front office. It should be a moment. The unboxing is when the coach — and the athletes — see their program's new identity for the first time.

**In-Person Unboxing (Ideal):**
- Schedule a time when the coach and key athletes can be present
- Open the boxes together
- Pull out the first jersey — watch the reaction
- Have the athletes try them on immediately
- Take photos (with permission) for social media and marketing
- Celebrate the moment — this is what "Built for the Program" looks like

**Remote Unboxing (When You Can't Be There):**
- Send the coach an unboxing guide
- Ask them to take photos/video
- Schedule a call for right after they open the boxes
- Be available for any questions or concerns

### 7.2 The Unboxing Script (In-Person)

> *"Coach, this is the moment. [X] weeks ago, we sat down and talked about what [School] [sport] could look like. Today, it's real. Let's open these boxes."*

*[Open box. Pull out first jersey. Hold it up.]*

> *"Coach — PRO CUT fit. See the taper? See how the sleeves articulate? This is what college programs wear. And now it's what [School] wears."*

*[Hand jersey to coach. Let them feel it. Let athletes try it on.]*

> *"How does it feel? How does it look?"*

*[Take photos. Celebrate.]*

> *"Coach — IRONCLAD GUARANTEE. If anything's not right, you call me. We fix it. Period. But looking at this — I think we got it right."*

### 7.3 The Unboxing Guide (For Remote Delivery)

Send this to the coach before delivery:

> **TUF UNBOXING GUIDE**
>
> Coach — your uniforms are arriving [date]. Here's how to make the most of the moment:
>
> 1. **Open one box first** — do a quick quality check before the full reveal
> 2. **Gather your captains/seniors** — let them be the first to see the new look
> 3. **Try them on immediately** — fit is everything. Make sure they feel right.
> 4. **Take photos** — we'd love to see the reveal. Send them our way or tag us.
> 5. **Call me** — I want to hear your reaction. Good, bad, anything. [Phone]
>
> Remember: IRONCLAD GUARANTEE. If anything's not right, we fix it. Period.

## PART 8: POST-DELIVERY FOLLOW-UP

### 8.1 The Satisfaction Check

7 days after delivery, conduct a satisfaction check. This is not a sales call. It's a relationship call.

**Script:**
> *"Coach, [Your Name] with TUF. Your athletes have been in the [Collection] uniforms for a week now. I wanted to check in — how's the fit? Any issues? What are the kids saying?"*

**What to Listen For:**
- Fit feedback (PRO CUT validation)
- Durability concerns (early warning sign)
- Athlete reactions (marketing gold, referral fuel)
- Any small issues that need addressing (proactive IRONCLAD)

### 8.2 The Satisfaction Check Outcomes

| Coach Says | Your Move |
|------------|-----------|
| "They're great. Kids love them." | Celebrate. Ask for a testimonial. Plant the seed for Lane 2 (travel gear). |
| "Mostly good, but one kid's jersey is a little tight." | "Let me send a replacement in the next size up. IRONCLAD GUARANTEE — we fix it." |
| "They're fine." (lukewarm) | "What would make them great? I want to make sure we earn your excitement." |
| "Actually, there's an issue..." | Listen. Diagnose. Fix. IRONCLAD GUARANTEE in action. |

### 8.3 The Testimonial Ask

If the coach is happy, ask for a testimonial:

> *"Coach, I'm glad you're happy with the uniforms. Would you be open to sharing that — a quick quote I can use? Other coaches trust other coaches more than they trust me. Your word would mean a lot."*

## PART 9: ISSUE RESOLUTION — THE IRONCLAD GUARANTEE IN ACTION

### 9.1 The IRONCLAD GUARANTEE Promise

> **"If anything goes wrong with the product, TUF fixes it. Period. Not pro-rated. Not 'within reason.' Fixed."**

This is the moment that promise becomes real. How you handle issues defines TUF's reputation more than any marketing ever could.

### 9.2 The Issue Resolution Protocol

| Step | Action | Timeline |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | **Acknowledge immediately.** When the coach reports an issue, respond within 2 hours. "Coach, I'm on it." | < 2 hours |
| 2 | **Own the problem.** Never say "the vendor messed up" or "Operations dropped the ball." You are TUF. The problem is yours to fix. | Immediate |
| 3 | **Diagnose.** What exactly is wrong? How many items? What's the impact? | < 24 hours |
| 4 | **Present the solution.** "Coach, here's what we're going to do: [specific fix]. Timeline: [date]." | < 24 hours |
| 5 | **Execute the fix.** Replace, repair, or refund. Whatever it takes. | As fast as possible |
| 6 | **Follow up.** After the fix: "Coach, is everything resolved to your satisfaction?" | < 48 hours after fix |

### 9.3 Issue Resolution Scripts

#### Script 1: Coach Reports a Problem

> *"Coach, thank you for telling me. I'm going to take care of this. Let me get the details — exactly what's wrong, which items, how many — and I'll have a solution for you by [end of day / tomorrow morning]. You have my word."*

#### Script 2: Presenting the Solution

> *"Coach, here's the plan: we're replacing [items] at no cost to you. They're being expedited — you'll have them by [date]. In the meantime, [workaround if applicable]. This is exactly what the IRONCLAD GUARANTEE is for. I'm sorry it happened, and we're fixing it."*

#### Script 3: After the Fix

> *"Coach, the replacement [items] should have arrived. Everything looking right now? I want to make sure you're 100% satisfied."*

### 9.4 Issue Categories and Standard Responses

| Issue | Standard Response |
|-------|------------------|
| **Wrong size (1–3 items)** | Reorder correct sizes. Expedite shipping. No charge. |
| **Wrong size (many items)** | Full assessment. Replace all incorrect sizes. Expedite. |
| **Color doesn't match** | If production error: full reorder at TUF's expense. If artwork error: determine who approved. Split cost or full reorder based on circumstances. |
| **Peeling graphics** | Replace immediately. Investigate production issue to prevent recurrence. |
| **Stitching failure** | Replace or repair. LOCK-FIT is a promise — this shouldn't happen. |
| **Missing items** | Ship immediately. Verify if they were missed in production or lost in transit. |
| **Late delivery** | Communicate proactively. Apologize sincerely. Offer something to make it right (discount on next order, free rush on next order, extra item). |
| **Defective fabric** | Replace all affected items. Return defective items to vendor for analysis. |

### 9.5 The IRONCLAD Rule

> **The cost of fixing a problem is the cost of keeping a customer. The cost of NOT fixing a problem is losing a customer — and every coach they talk to.**

## PART 10: UPSELL DURING DELIVERY — INTRODUCING THE NEXT LANE

### 10.1 The Timing of Upsell

The best time to introduce the next lane is when the coach is most excited about the current one. That's usually:

- At the unboxing, when they see the uniforms for the first time
- One week after delivery, when the athletes have worn them and the feedback is positive
- After the first game, when the program looks sharp and the coach is proud

### 10.2 Lane Expansion Scripts

#### Uniform to Travel Gear (Lane 1 → Lane 2)

> *"Coach, your athletes look sharp in GRIND on Friday nights. Here's my question: what are they wearing when they get off the bus at an away game? Because our ISSUE travel gear — tech suits, duffles, backpacks — is built to the same quality standard and same design language as your uniforms. PACKSYSTEM integration. Your team competes in a system. They should travel in a system. Want me to put together a travel package?"*

#### Uniform to Team Store (Lane 1 → Lane 3)

> *"Coach, I was at your game last Friday. The stands were packed. Here's what I noticed: your fans were wearing random school T-shirts. Imagine if they could buy gear that actually matches what your athletes wear — same quality, same design language. Our team store model: online storefront, program-branded gear, your program earns 15–25% on every sale. It's revenue you're leaving on the table. Want to see how it works?"*

#### Travel Gear to Letterman Jackets (Lane 2 → Lane 4)

> *"Coach, your seniors have been in the TUF system — competing in GRIND, traveling in ISSUE. They've earned LEGACY. Our letterman jackets — premium wool, leather sleeves, custom chenille patches. The jacket that carries your program's history. Let's make sure your seniors get what they've earned."*

#### Single Sport to Multi-Sport (Within Lane 1)

> *"Coach, you're TUF in football. What about basketball? OVERTIME collection. FLEX-WAIST shorts — solves the waistband problem nobody else has solved. Your basketball coach is going to see what your football team looks like and ask why they don't have it. Let's get ahead of that conversation."*

### 10.3 The Upsell Rule

> **Never pitch the next lane until the current lane is delivered and the coach is happy. Upsell from satisfaction, not from anticipation.**

## PART 11: REFERRAL REQUESTS

### 11.1 When to Ask for Referrals

The ideal referral window opens when the coach is happiest with TUF:

- After a successful delivery and unboxing
- After the first game in the new uniforms (if the coach is excited)
- After you've resolved an issue exceptionally well (IRONCLAD in action)
- When the coach unprompted says something positive

### 11.2 Referral Scripts

#### Script 1: The Direct Ask (Delivery)

> *"Coach, I'm glad you're happy with the uniforms. Let me ask: is there another coach in your conference who's dealing with the same uniform frustrations you had before TUF? Someone who might appreciate seeing what you've got? I'd love an introduction."*

#### Script 2: The Soft Ask (Satisfaction Check)

> *"Coach, I'm not asking you to be a salesperson. But if you're at a coaching clinic and someone complains about their uniforms — boxy fit, late delivery, peeling graphics — I'd appreciate you mentioning our name. That's all."*

#### Script 3: The Conference Play

> *"Coach, you're in the [Conference Name]. That's [X] other schools. I'd bet at least half of them are dealing with vendor frustrations. If there's one coach you'd feel good about introducing me to, I'd be grateful."*

### 11.3 Referral Follow-Up

When a coach gives you a referral:

1. Thank them immediately — a handwritten note, not just an email
2. Ask for context: "What should I know about Coach [Name]?"
3. Contact the referral within 48 hours
4. Mention the referring coach: "Coach [Name] from [School] suggested I reach out"
5. Update the referring coach after you connect

## PART 12: 10 ORDER HANDOFF SCENARIOS

### Instructions

Practice these with your Director. One plays Rep, one plays Coach. Focus on the handoff process, communication, and issue resolution.

---

### Scenario 1: The Smooth Handoff

**Situation:** Coach signed the order. Deposit paid. All information complete. Everything is ready to submit to Operations.

**Rep's Task:** Walk through the handoff checklist. Set timeline expectations. Send the confirmation email.

---

### Scenario 2: Missing Logo Files

**Situation:** Coach signed the order but hasn't sent the logo files. The artwork can't be finalized without them.

**Rep's Task:** Get the logo files. Don't submit an incomplete order to Operations.

**Script:**
> *"Coach, I need one more thing before I can submit your order — your logo files. Vector format if you have it (AI, EPS), or a high-resolution PNG (300dpi+). Who at the school handles that — the AD, the yearbook advisor, the communications person? Once I have those, we're in production."*

---

### Scenario 3: The Incomplete Roster

**Situation:** Coach provided the roster but 5 athletes are missing sizes.

**Rep's Task:** Get the missing sizes. Offer a sizing session if needed.

---

### Scenario 4: The Production Delay

**Situation:** Operations informs you that production is running 2 weeks behind schedule. The coach's delivery date will slip.

**Rep's Task:** Tell the coach proactively. Apologize. Provide the new timeline. Don't wait for them to ask.

**Script:**
> *"Coach, I need to be straight with you. We hit a production delay — [specific reason]. Your new estimated delivery is [new date] — that's [X weeks] later than we planned. I know that's not ideal, and I'm sorry. Here's what I'm doing: I've flagged your order as priority. I'm checking in weekly instead of at the midpoint. And I'll keep you posted every step. If there's anything you need in the meantime — loaner uniforms, partial shipment — let's figure it out."*

---

### Scenario 5: The Wrong Size Arrives

**Situation:** Uniforms delivered. Coach finds 3 jerseys in the wrong size.

**Rep's Task:** Own it. Fix it. IRONCLAD in action.

**Script:**
> *"Coach, I'm sorry about that. Those three jerseys — I'm reordering right now in the correct sizes. Expedited. They'll be here in [X] days. In the meantime, we've got the sizes you need if there's one close. This is exactly what the IRONCLAD GUARANTEE is for. We fix it. Period."*

---

### Scenario 6: The Missing Box

**Situation:** Coach received the shipment, but one box is missing. Half the away jerseys aren't there.

**Rep's Task:** Track the missing box. Determine if it was missed in production or lost in shipping. Communicate clearly.

---

### Scenario 7: The Unhappy Coach (Color Issue)

**Situation:** Coach says the red on the jerseys is "too orange." It doesn't match the school colors.

**Rep's Task:** Determine the root cause. Was it an artwork error (coach approved wrong color) or production error? Resolve based on cause.

---

### Scenario 8: The Unboxing Event

**Situation:** You're at the school for the unboxing. The coach is excited. The team captains are there.

**Rep's Task:** Make it a moment. Demo the fit. Get photos. Plant the seed for travel gear.

---

### Scenario 9: The Lane Expansion Conversation

**Situation:** One week after delivery. Coach is happy. You're on the satisfaction check call.

**Rep's Task:** Confirm satisfaction. Transition to Lane 2 (ISSUE travel gear).

---

### Scenario 10: The Referral Request

**Situation:** Delivery was perfect. Coach loves the uniforms. You're wrapping up the satisfaction check.

**Rep's Task:** Ask for a referral. Get a name. Get context.
