PART 1: THE PRODUCT KNOWLEDGE MINDSET

1.1 Knowing Specs vs. Embodying the Brand

There's a difference between knowing what POWER-STRETCH is and being able to hold up a jersey, stretch the fabric in four directions, and make a coach feel the difference.

Knows SpecsEmbodies the Brand
"PRO CUT is our athletic-tapered fit with articulated sleeves and reduced seam volume."Holds up jersey next to competitor. "Coach, see the difference? PRO CUT. Built to what college programs actually wear. Your athletes feel it immediately."
"POWER-STRETCH is our 4-way stretch fabric with shape recovery."Stretches fabric in four directions. "POWER-STRETCH. Moves with the athlete. Snaps back. Doesn't bag out after a season. Feel it."
"The 4-lane system includes uniforms, travel gear, team stores, and letterman jackets.""Coach, right now you're juggling four vendors. We do all four. One system. One look. One call. That's the difference between buying uniforms and building your program's identity."

Know the left column cold. Deploy the right column in the field.

1.2 The Product Knowledge Principles

Principle 1: The Demo Proves the Claim — Never say a standard name without showing it physically.

Principle 2: Three Standards, Not Seven — Deploy exactly three: PRO CUT (lead), sport-specific standard, IRONCLAD GUARANTEE (close).

Principle 3: Collections Are Identities — GRIND isn't "the football jersey." It's "what your football program wears when the work meets the field."

Principle 4: Honesty Builds Credibility — TUF doesn't claim to have invented polyester or moisture-wicking. We claim quality standards — named thresholds our vendors must meet.

Principle 5: Know What We Don't Claim — We set standards. Nike names Dri-FIT. Adidas names AEROREADY. TUF plays the same game — with the same legitimacy.
