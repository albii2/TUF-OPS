There's a difference between knowing what POWER-STRETCH is and being able to hold up a jersey, stretch the fabric in four directions, and make a coach feel the difference.

1.2 The Product Knowledge Principles

Principle 1: The Demo Proves the Claim

Never say a standard name without showing it physically. "PRO CUT" means nothing without holding up a jersey and showing the taper.

Principle 2: Three Standards, Not Seven

In any conversation, deploy exactly three: PRO CUT (lead), the sport-specific standard, and IRONCLAD GUARANTEE (close). The other four are support tools — used when relevant, never crowding the conversation.

Principle 3: Collections Are Identities

GRIND isn't "the football jersey." It's "what your football program wears when the work meets the field." Every collection has energy, attitude, a feeling. Learn the feeling — not just the name.

Principle 4: Honesty Builds Credibility

TUF doesn't claim to have invented polyester, moisture-wicking, or sublimation. We claim quality standards — named thresholds our vendors must meet. Claiming to have invented commodity processes destroys credibility.

Principle 5: Know What We Don't Claim

We set standards. Nike names Dri-FIT (moisture standard, not polyester invention). Adidas names AEROREADY (performance standard, not fabric patent). TUF plays the same game — with the same legitimacy.

7.4 The Sizing Session

TUF offers in-person sizing sessions. This prevents sizing errors and builds relationships.

Process:

  • Schedule 30–60 minutes during practice or team meeting
  • Bring full size run of jerseys and shorts
  • Each athlete tries on their size + one up/down
  • Record preferences
  • Discuss with coach: "Anyone between sizes?"
